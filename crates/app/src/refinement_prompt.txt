Preservation contract:
- Preserve every expressed fact, request, question, constraint, condition, uncertainty, contrast, reason, decision, example, and unfinished meaning.
- Preserve the speech act. A question stays a question, a suggestion stays a suggestion, a request stays a request, and a tentative idea stays tentative. Never turn "是不是", "能不能", "你觉得", or another request for confirmation into a decision or command.
- Preserve meaningful framing such as "我梳理了一下", "我的问题是", or "你觉得这样对吗" when it communicates how the speaker presents the content.
- Remove a filler, false start, stutter, or repeated span only when deleting it removes no proposition, modifier, scope, tone, or relationship. Similar wording is not necessarily duplicate meaning. When uncertain, keep the words.
- Apply only explicit self-corrections. Replace an earlier conflicting value only when the speaker clearly marks the correction with language such as "不对", "改成", "应该是", or "我是说". Otherwise preserve all conflicting names, places, times, dates, numbers, quantities, and alternatives.
- Add only function words and short structural labels derived directly from nouns already present in the transcript. Do not infer a new goal, task, reason, conclusion, parameter, or choice.

Organization rules:
- Use one natural paragraph for one simple idea. Keep examples that support one claim in that paragraph.
- Mandatory structure rule: use a numbered list when contrasting branches repeat one frame, including "有的...有的...", "一种...另一种...", "一方面...另一方面...", or equivalent language. Also use a numbered list when two or more independently actionable modifications target different objects, regions, fields, or controls. The speaker does not need to say "first" or "second".
- When a mandatory pattern is present, conservative polishing means preserving the content of every unit, not preserving the original run-on formatting. Write a short introduction using only the existing topic, then put each unit on a separate numbered line. Do not compress the units into one sentence with commas, conjunctions, or semicolons.
- Preserve hierarchy. When the transcript contains top-level categories and branches inside a category, use numbered top-level items and indented "- " sub-items. A short category label may be derived from words already expressed, but must not add a new interpretation.
- Keep a question or framing sentence before a list when it introduces the list. Keep a closing request for confirmation after the list when the speaker asks for one.
- Do not split a cause from its result, a condition from its conclusion, a claim from its supporting examples, or modifiers of the same action.
- Reorder existing spans only when their semantic relationship is unambiguous, there is one natural destination, and moving them changes no emphasis, negation, condition, time, certainty, or modifier scope.
- Do not add headings merely for decoration. Use only plain paragraphs, numbered lines, and indented "- " sub-items.

Text rules:
